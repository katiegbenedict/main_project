PANTONE'S Colors of the Year

Every year since 2000, Pantone has
selected a color that has been
specifically influential in that
particular year. Whether it be in the
entertainment industry, fashion
industry, popular art trends, travel
destinations, or the current status
of society, Pantone takes everything
into consideration when selecting a
color.

Pantone’s color of the year is
widely influential in all realms
of design, including fashion,
product, interior, and graphic
design.

2000

CERULEAN

Color of the millennium,
Cerulean is the first-ever
Pantone Color of the Year.

Heading into the new
century filled with
uncertainty, Cerulean,
similar to a clear blue
sky, brings peace,
calmness, and serenity.

A reminder of the
outdoors, the sky, and
water, Cerulean
provides stress relief
and relaxation at times
when it is needed most.

2001

FUCHSIA ROSE

Fuchsia Rose was
chosen as 2001’s color
of the year largely in part
because it is essentially
the opposite of 2000’s
cerulean.

Intense, bright, and
passionate, Fuchsia
Rose attracts many.

2002

TRUE RED

2002’s color, True Red,
was chosen as a
remembrance of the
events from 9/11.

Honoring the fallen
and the courage of
those who protected,
the powerful hue
represents love and
compassion.

2003

AQUA SKY

A calm and cool tone,
Aqua Sky was chosen
as 2003’s Color of the
Year.

Aqua Sky brings a sense of hope and
serenity, represents inner peace, and
encourages healing.

The first blue tone of
Pantone’s Colors of the Year,
Aqua Sky starts a trend of
many blues in the years to
come.

2004

TIGERLILY

Along with 2004 came the
trend of orange. The bright
and exotic yellow and red
mix is named after the
Tigerlily flower, representing
nature’s influence in design.

2005

BLUE TURQUOISE

Continuing with inspiration
from nature, 2005’s color is
Blue Turquoise. The color
of the sea, Blue Turquoise
is calm and cool, providing
serenity and tranquility.

2006

SAND DOLLAR

2006 brought the first neutral
tone to Pantone’s Color of the
Year collection.

The beige and subtle Sand
Dollar represents the
economic difficulties the
world was experiencing at
this time.

Not only is it symbolic
of the economic status
of the world, but was
very popular in both
fashion and interior
design.

2007

CHILI PEPPER

The big and bold color,
Chilli Pepper, was chosen
because of the way it
demands attention.

The fiery and passionate
color evokes power and
expressiveness and is
used often for self-
expression and making
personal statements.

2008

BLUE IRIS

Blue Iris was chosen for 2008’s
Color of the Year due to its stable
and calm undertones as well as
the calm state it evokes.

The slight addition of
purple to the blue hue
adds a sliver of
excitement while still
providing a sense of
reassurance in a
complex time.

2009

MIMOSA

Named after the flower
as well as the orange
juice drink, Mimosa
was chosen for 2009.

The cheery yellow radiates
warmth and optimism in a time
when people were craving it.

The dynamic color brought
energy and encouraged
innovation and inspiration.

2010

TURQUOISE

Slightly more green than
Blue Turquoise, Turquoise
was chosen due to its
inviting and soothing, yet
bright hue. A reminder of
a tropical vacation, turquoise
brings a sense of calmness,
relief, and comfort.

Often meaning compassion,
healing, faith, serenity,
tranquility, and patience,
turquoise often takes
people on a mental vacation.

Turquoise often results in a
positive reaction and works
well in interior design, pairing
nicely with many other colors,
whether it be other blues, reds
and pinks, greens, or even
yellows.

2011

HONEYSUCKLE

While Turquoise brought us on
vacation, honeysuckle brings us
back to reality. With its bright,
confident, and encouraging
properties, honeysuckle motivates
and drives people forward.

“Honeysuckle is a captivating, stimulating
color that gets the adrenaline going –
perfect to ward off the blues. The intensity
of this festive reddish pink allures and
engages. In fact, this color, not the sweet
fragrance of the flower blossoms for which
it was named, is what attracts
hummingbirds to nectar.”

- Leatrice Eisemen
Executive Director of
Pantone Color Institute

2012

TANGERINE TANGO

Tangerine Tango was chosen
largely for its vibrant hue and
the energy it provides.

“Sophisticated but at the same
time dramatic and seductive,
Tangerine Tango is an orange with
a lot of depth to it.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

“Reminiscent of the radiant shadings
of a sunset, Tangerine Tango marries
the vivaciousness and adrenaline
rush of red with the friendliness and
warmth of yellow, to form a high-
visibility, magnetic hue that emanates
heat and energy.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

2013

EMERALD

Calming down from 2012’s
Tangerine Tango, Emerald
provides a sense of wellbeing,
balance, and harmony.

The vivid green is often
associated with the
gemstone, representing
luxury, sophistication,
and beauty.

The most prevalent color
in nature, emerald green
resembles growth and
often brings a sense of
clarity.

2014

RADIANT ORCHID

Representing the exotic and
bright flower, radiant orchid was
seen an extensive amount on
runways as well as the red
carpet in 2014.

The color was chosen not
for the feelings it evokes,
but rather due to its versatility
in clothing, makeup, and
interior design.

The light purple complements
many skin tones, hair colors,
and eye colors. Radiant orchid
also works well in interior
spaces, complementing most
colors, and bringing energy to
many color palettes.

4

2015

MARSALA

An earthy and natural color,
Marsala was chosen largely
for its versatility in fashion,
beauty, and interiors, much
like 2014’s Radiant Orchid.

The natural and dark wine-red
appeals to a large crowd for
both clothing and accessories,
as well as lipstick or nail polish.

The color brings a sense of
warmth and sophistication
to spaces.

2016

ROSE QUARTZ/
SERENITY

2016 brought something new
to Pantone’s colors of the year:
the blending of two colors for
one year.

“Joined together Rose Quartz
and Serenity demonstrate
an inherent balance between
a warmer embracing rose
tone and the cooler tranquil
blue, reflecting connection
and wellness as well as a
soothing sense of order and
peace.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

The blending of these two colors
is symbolic of the blurred gender
lines that became prevalent in
2016 in regards to clothing and
fashion.

2017

GREENERY

“Greenery bursts forth in 2017
to provide us with the
reassurance we yearn for amid
a tumultuous social and political
environment.”

“Satisfying our growing desire to
rejuvenate and revitalize, Greenery
symbolizes the reconnection we
seek with nature, one another, and
a larger purpose.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

This color suggests a break
from modernity and encourages
engagement with nature,
representing revitalization
and vitality.

2018

ULTRA VIOLET

Dramatic and mysterious, Ultra
Violet points us toward the future.

“From exploring new technologies
and the greater galaxy, to artistic
expression and spiritual reflection,
intuitive Ultra Violet lights the way
to what is to come.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

Used often by artists such as
Prince or David Bowie, Ultra
Violet is commonly used for
self-expression.

2019

LIVING CORAL

“With consumers craving
human interaction and social
connection, the humanizing
and heartening qualities
displayed by the convivial
PANTONE Living Coral hit a
responsive chord.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

As a color seen both in
nature and in the digital
realm, Living Coral
brings authenticity and
intimacy to a world that
relies solely on social
media and technology.

2020

CLASSIC BLUE

Elegant, thought-provoking,
and dependable, Classic
Blue brings a sense of
calmness and security.

“Imbued with a deep resonance,
Classic Blue provides an
anchoring foundation. A
boundless blue evocative of the
vast and evening sky, Classic
Blue encourages us to look
beyond the obvious and expand
our thinking.”

-Leatrice Eisemen

2021

ULTIMATE GRAY/
ILLUMINATING

After such a strange and
unexpected 2020, 2021 brought
two colors: Ultimate Gray and
Illuminating.

“The union of an enduring
Ultimate Gray with the vibrant
yellow Illuminating expresses a
message of positivity supported
by fortitude. Practical and rock-
solid but at the same time
warming and optimistic, this is a
color combination that gives us
resilience and hope.”

-Leatrice Eisemen
